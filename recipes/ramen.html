Ramen

This quick and easy Parmesan garlic ramen satisfies the craving for an Italian-style noodle dish, and it is super budget-friendly. Fresh garlic, a little grated Parmesan, and Italian seasoning, and instant ramen can feel a bit like Italy.

ramen History

Ramen, a staple of Japanese fast food, has roots in Chinese noodle dishes that were introduced to Japan in the 19th century. It evolved into a unique Japanese dish, featuring wheat noodles in a flavorful soup, often flavored with soy sauce, miso, or other seasonings. Instant ramen, a popular form of ramen, was invented in 1958 by Momofuku Ando, who developed the first mass-produced instant ramen, later introducing Cup Noodles in 1971.

Ingredients need to make ramen

  • 3 (3-ounce) packages instant ramen noodles, seasoning packets discarded
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning

step by step guid to mkae tasty garlic ramen

  1. Bring 6 cups water to a boil in a large soup pot; add noodles and boil for 3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Drain, reserving 1 cup of cooking, water and set aside.
  2. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Stir in Parmesan cheese, Italian seasoning, and reserved cooking water. Cook until cheese is melted and sauce is thick, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add noodles to the skillet, and toss gently until heated through . Top with parsley if desired, and serve immediately.
